## Goal

Write a personal narrative blog post about Fraser Burns' transition from Quality Control Inspector to Quality Team Leader -what the shift actually felt like, what surprised him, and what he'd tell someone considering the same move.

---

## Structure

### 1. Opening hook

A specific moment that illustrated the difference between the two roles -the instinct to go fix something yourself and having to stop. Grounds the post in a real, relatable experience immediately.

### 2. The mindset shift

Moving from "find the problem" to "build the system that prevents it." The job looks similar from the outside (quality is quality) but the orientation is completely different. As an inspector you are reactive -as a team leader you are responsible for whether the right conditions exist in the first place.

### 3. What surprised him

The soft skills that matter more than expected:

- Having difficult conversations (with the team, with management, with other departments)
- Trusting the team to do the work without stepping in
- Communicating upward -translating quality issues into language that makes sense to people who don't live in it

### 4. What carried over

The skills from inspection that proved more valuable than expected in a leadership role:

- The inspector's eye for detail -knowing what "good" looks like
- The habit of asking "why" until you get to the real cause
- Credibility with the team that comes from having done the job yourself

### 5. Closing thought

What Fraser would tell himself before making the move -honest, grounded, forward-looking.
