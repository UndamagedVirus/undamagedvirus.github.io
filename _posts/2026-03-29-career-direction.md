I am a Quality Team Leader. That is where I am right now, and on most days it is where my energy goes: maintaining and updating the QMS, making sure the team has what they need, working to build a culture where quality is not an afterthought but part of how things are done here.

It is good work. But it is not the destination.

## Where I Want to Get To

The goal is Quality Manager. Not because of the title, but because of what it means in practice: the ability to shape how an organisation approaches quality at a level where it actually sticks. To influence processes, culture, and standards in a way that makes a real difference to how people work and what customers receive.

That is what I am working toward.

## The Roadmap

I am deliberate about how I get there. These are the qualifications I am working through and why each one matters:

**HNC Mechanical Engineering** (in progress) - the technical foundation. Understanding the engineering context behind the quality work I do every day.

**Lead Auditor** - learning to assess quality systems from the outside in. Auditing disciplines your thinking and sharpens your ability to identify where a system is strong and where it is not.

**ASQ Certified Quality Manager** - a recognised professional standard from one of the most respected quality bodies in the world. This is about demonstrating that the knowledge is real and tested, not just accumulated on the job.

**ASQ Quality Engineer** - deepening the technical side. Quality engineering sits at the intersection of process, data, and improvement, and I want to be competent across all of it.

**CQI Chartered Status** - the professional benchmark here in the UK. Chartered membership of the Chartered Quality Institute represents a commitment to the profession and to continuous development within it.

None of these are boxes to tick. Each one represents something I want to understand better and be able to apply.

## Why It Matters

Quality done well changes how organisations work. It is not just about catching defects or closing non-conformances. It is about building systems and cultures where problems are prevented in the first place, where people have the clarity and support to do their jobs properly, and where the standard is maintained because everyone understands why it matters.

That is worth committing to. It is the kind of professional I want to be.

I have seen what a well-run quality function looks like and what it does for the people working within it. I have also seen the opposite. The difference is significant, and it does not happen by accident. It takes people who are genuinely invested in getting it right.

## The Path Is Long

I am not going to pretend this happens quickly. The qualifications take time, the experience has to be earned, and building something worth building at Scott & Fyfe is itself an ongoing project.

But the direction is set. The reasons are clear. That is enough to keep going.
